Reading with a clear purpose

TheoryReading
2Before reading: know what to look for

Leer en inglés no significa traducir todo palabra por palabra. Primero necesitas saber qué busca la tarea. Si la tarea pide idea general, no debes perderte en detalles menores. Si pide información específica, debes rastrear nombres, fechas, lugares, requisitos o datos puntuales. Si pide propósito o audiencia, debes observar tono, registro y situación comunicativa.

En esta guía el foco es write a text on a topic of interest following a discursive structure. Eso significa que cada palabra útil se interpreta dentro de una función: informar, persuadir, describir, comparar, resumir o expresar identidad.

Language focus in context

TheoryLanguage
3Useful language: topic sentence, supporting reasons, example and final idea

El vocabulario y las estructuras gramaticales se aprenden mejor cuando sabes para qué sirven. En esta guía, el foco lingüístico es topic sentence, supporting reasons, example and final idea. No lo estudies como lista aislada: úsalo para construir significado.

Una estructura clara en inglés suele responder a esta ruta: subject + verb + idea + support. Por ejemplo: Students use English to share local problems because they want international audiences to understand their reality.

Text type and structure

TheoryText structure
4How the text type works

Every text type organizes information in a different way. A review presents an opinion and evidence; an announcement gives details quickly; an opinion paragraph presents a topic sentence and reasons; a table organizes information by categories.

For this guide, the text type is opinion paragraph. Before answering, identify its parts. That will help you understand not only what the text says, but also why it is written that way.

Model input: Music is part of my identity because it connects memories, emotions and language. For example, some songs remind me of my family, while others help me understand different cultures.

Common mistakes and better strategies

TheoryStrategy
5What usually goes wrong

Many students fail English tasks not because they know nothing, but because they use the wrong strategy. They translate too much, ignore the task, copy without understanding, or write sentences without a complete idea.

Common mistakes

1. Translating every word. 2. Ignoring the text type. 3. Writing without subject and verb. 4. Choosing an answer that sounds familiar but is not supported by the text. 5. Forgetting audience and purpose.

Weak strategyBetter strategyWhy it works
Translate everything.Look for purpose and keywords first.You read with direction.
Copy a sentence randomly.Choose evidence that answers the task.Your answer becomes justified.
Write disconnected words.Use subject + verb + complete idea.Your English communicates clearly.
